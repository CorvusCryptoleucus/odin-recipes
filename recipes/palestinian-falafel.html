Palestinian Falafel

Description

This traditional Palestinian Falafel recipe brings the rich and authentic flavors of the Middle East to your kitchen. Made from soaked chickpeas, fresh herbs like parsley and cilantro, and seasoned with aromatic spices such as cumin and coriander, these falafels are perfectly crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. They are typically served hot with pita bread, tahini sauce, hummus, fresh vegetables, and pickles, making for a delightful and satisfying meal. Enjoy the vibrant taste and texture of homemade falafel, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (not canned)
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1-2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (as needed)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas:
    • Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover with plenty of cold water. Let them soak overnight, or for at least 12 hours. They will double in size. Drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.
  2. Make the Falafel Mixture:
    • In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, parsley, cilantro, onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, black pepper, salt, cayenne pepper (if using), baking soda, and lemon juice.
    • Process the mixture until it is well combined but still slightly coarse in texture. You want it to stick together but not be too smooth.
    • If the mixture is too wet, add 1-2 tablespoons of flour to help bind it.
  3. Shape the Falafel:
    • Using your hands or a falafel scoop, form the mixture into small balls or patties, about the size of a walnut. Place them on a baking sheet or plate.
  4. Fry the Falafel:
    • Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C).
    • Carefully drop the falafel balls into the hot oil, a few at a time, and fry until they are golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan.
    • Remove the falafel with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the falafel hot with pita bread, tahini sauce, hummus, fresh vegetables, and pickles. They can also be enjoyed as a snack or appetizer.

Optional Tahini Sauce

  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  • In a bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, water, garlic, and salt until smooth and creamy. Adjust the consistency with more water or lemon juice if needed.

This traditional Palestinian falafel recipe is sure to bring authentic Middle Eastern flavors to your table. Enjoy!
