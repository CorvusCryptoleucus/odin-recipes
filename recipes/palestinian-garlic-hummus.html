Palestinian Garlic Hummus

Description

This Palestinian Garlic Hummus recipe offers a creamy and flavorful dip perfect for any occasion. Made from a blend of cooked chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and fresh garlic, this hummus is both nutritious and delicious. The addition of cumin and extra virgin olive oil enhances the depth of flavor, while the option to garnish with fresh parsley and paprika adds a touch of color and extra taste. Serve it with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or as a spread in sandwiches for a delightful treat that’s sure to please everyone.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup cold water (more if needed)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Paprika for garnish
  • Additional olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas:
    • If using dried chickpeas, place them in a large bowl and cover with water. Add 1 teaspoon of baking soda and soak overnight. Drain and rinse the chickpeas the next day.
    • If using canned chickpeas, skip the soaking and rinsing step.
  2. Cook the Chickpeas:
    • In a large pot, add the soaked chickpeas and cover with several inches of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 1-1.5 hours until the chickpeas are very tender. If using canned chickpeas, this step takes about 20 minutes.
    • Drain the chickpeas, reserving some of the cooking liquid.
  3. Blend the Hummus:
    • In a food processor, combine the tahini and lemon juice. Process for about 1 minute until well blended and creamy.
    • Add the minced garlic, cumin, and a pinch of salt to the tahini mixture. Process for another 30 seconds to mix well.
    • Add the cooked chickpeas to the food processor. Process for about 1-2 minutes until smooth and creamy. You may need to stop and scrape down the sides of the bowl occasionally.
  4. Adjust the Consistency:
    • While the food processor is running, slowly add cold water or the reserved cooking liquid (one tablespoon at a time) until the hummus reaches your desired consistency.
    • Taste and adjust the seasoning with additional salt, lemon juice, or garlic if needed.
  5. Serve:
    • Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and sprinkle with paprika.
    • Garnish with chopped fresh parsley.
  6. Enjoy:
    • Serve the hummus with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or use as a spread in sandwiches.

This recipe makes a creamy and flavorful Palestinian Garlic Hummus that's perfect for any occasion. Enjoy!
