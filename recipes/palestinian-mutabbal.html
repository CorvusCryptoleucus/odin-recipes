Palestinian Mutabbal

Description

Mutabbal is a flavorful Middle Eastern dip similar to baba ghanoush, but with a creamier texture and a stronger garlic flavor. It is made primarily from roasted eggplant, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil. Mutabbal is a popular dish in Palestinian cuisine, often served as part of mezze platters or as a side dish.p>

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Pomegranate seeds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Prick the eggplant with a fork in several places. Place it on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  3. Remove the eggplant from the oven and let it cool. Once cool enough to handle, peel off and discard the charred skin.
  4. In a bowl, mash the roasted eggplant flesh with a fork or blend it briefly in a food processor for a smoother texture.
  5. Add minced garlic, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, and salt to the mashed eggplant. Mix well until everything is combined and smooth.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  7. Transfer the mutabbal to a serving dish. Drizzle with a bit more olive oil and garnish with chopped parsley and optional pomegranate seeds.
  8. Serve at room temperature with pita bread, crackers, or vegetable sticks.

Mutabbal is a delicious and creamy dip that showcases the rich flavors of roasted eggplant and tahini. Its garlic-infused taste makes it a delightful addition to any Middle Eastern-inspired spread or as a flavorful side dish.
